# IA Agent Bible v4.0

## Mission
Reimagine India's future through believable AI-powered restoration while preserving local identity.

## Non-negotiable Rules
1. Restore the experience, never the identity.
2. One hero object, instantly recognizable (<1 second).
3. Restore the complete surrounding ecosystem.
4. Hero occupies 65–75% of every frame.
5. 80% of construction directly touches the hero.
6. 250–600 active workers only.
7. No idle machinery.
8. No fantasy architecture.
9. No readable signs, logos or political posters.
10. Preserve regional identity.
11. Mix drone scale + ground emotion.
12. Entire neighbourhood must visibly improve.

## 28s Story
Hook → F1 Reality → F2 Mobilization → F3 Peak Engineering → F4 Near Completion → F5 Masterpiece → 8s Hero Reveal.

## Camera
Frames 1–5: Locked camera.
Hero Reveal: Drone Descent → Ground Tracking → Low Tracking → Drone Pullback.

## Construction Lifecycle
Reality → Mobilization → Peak Engineering → Finishing → Public Activation.

## QA
Hero visible.
Identity preserved.
Ecosystem restored.
No side distraction.
Engineering touches hero.
SEO complete.

## Learnings

## v9.0 Addendum - Continuous Motion & Temporal Grounding Protocol (Mandatory)

### Mission
Every ImagineIndia video must feel like one uninterrupted documentary recording spanning months of real engineering work, not five independent scenes. Every frame is a continuation of physical motion, never a new disconnected state.

### 1. Continuous Motion Principle
Every object visible on screen must have a believable physical history. Nothing may appear, disappear, relocate, or complete itself without showing the motion that caused it. The viewer should feel the camera kept recording, not that AI generated a new frame.

### 2. Temporal Grounding Rule
Every prompt must describe continuous actions, not completed results.

Never write: "Workers are present."
Write: "Workers continuously walk into the site from the surrounding streets."

Never write: "Excavator is on site."
Write: "The excavator slowly drives into the work zone under its own power."

Never write: "Road completed."
Write: "Road paving continuously expands outward as paving crews install each section."

Every visible change must have an observable cause.

### 3. Arrival Rule
People, machinery, materials, barricades, equipment, inspection teams, citizens, tourists, and vendors must never spawn inside the frame. Every entity must physically enter from surrounding streets, background roads, side lanes, or previously visible work areas.

### 4. Departure Rule
Nothing disappears. Heavy machinery exits by driving away. Workers walk out. Scaffolding is dismantled section by section. Barricades are progressively removed. Construction material is gradually transported away.

### 5. Progressive Engineering Rule
Engineering progresses continuously: excavation becomes trenching, trenching becomes utilities, utilities become backfilling, backfilling becomes paving, paving becomes polishing, polishing becomes public space, and public space becomes activated. No phase may be skipped.

### 6. Motion Before Result
Never ask for a completed condition. Always describe the motion creating that condition.

Wrong: "The garbage is gone."
Correct: "Garbage continuously decreases because workers, loaders, and trucks remove it throughout the sequence."

Wrong: "Landscaping completed."
Correct: "Landscape crews continuously plant shrubs, install soil, water trees, and expand planting beds."

### 7. Frame-to-Frame Handoff Rule
Every frame must finish by naturally preparing the next frame.

Reality to Mobilization: pollution continues; only in the final moments do surveyors arrive, workers walk into frame, and machinery approaches. No construction yet.

Mobilization to Peak Engineering: construction has already started; engineering intensity increases naturally; more machinery enters; utilities expand; road works spread.

Peak Engineering to Finishing: heavy construction gradually reduces; excavators leave completed zones; scaffolding comes down; lighting installation begins; street furniture appears only through installation.

Finishing to Completion: cleaning continues; lighting adjustments continue; workers perform inspections; materials leave through transport; citizens slowly occupy completed spaces.

Completion to Hero Reveal: the site is already complete; workers remain visible performing genuine finishing work; the drone continues documenting the finished project. No magical reveal.

### 8. Continuous Occupancy Rule
The site must never suddenly become empty. Workers reduce gradually, machinery reduces gradually, and citizens increase gradually. The ratio changes naturally over time.

### 9. Documentary Camera Rule
The camera is a passive observer. It never hides continuity. The camera records; the world changes.

### 10. Hero Reveal Rule
The Hero Reveal is the documentary's closing cinematic sequence, not another restoration frame. Its first seconds continue exactly where Frame 5 ended. Only then may the drone lift off in continuous motion: ground shot to slow ascent to forward motion to orbital reveal to wide establishing shot. No jump cuts, teleportation, or instant aerial view.

### 11. Reality Rule
Frame 1 establishes the cause, not merely the effect. Pollution must be actively created by ongoing human behaviour so the audience understands why the location deteriorated before restoration begins.

### 12. Civil Engineer Test Expanded
If a civil engineer pauses the video at any second, they must be able to explain what every worker is doing, why every machine is there, where every machine came from, what construction phase is occurring, what phase just finished, and what phase begins next.

If any object appears without believable arrival, or any improvement exists without visible work creating it, the prompt fails.

### ImagineIndia Golden Rule
Nothing appears. Everything arrives. Nothing disappears. Everything leaves. Nothing completes. Everything is continuously built.

This principle governs every image prompt, every image-to-video prompt, and every hero reveal across the ImagineIndia pipeline.
